What is Formula 1?

Formula 1 is an international form of auto racing, whose races are called Grand Prix.

The main aim of this sport is to optimise the car against the competition, to finish

the race in the highest position possible. People say that this sport is the "Pinnacle of Motorsports".

F1 teams and their drivers travel around the world competing for two championship titles:

the Drivers' Championship and the Constructors' Championship.

Why is Formula 1 called “Formula 1”?

Formula 1 is called "Formula" because the cars must adhere to a specific set of technical

regulations, or "formula," set by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA).

The "1" signifies that this is the premier, highest category of formula racing,

the most advanced and prestigious form of single-seater open-wheel racing available.
